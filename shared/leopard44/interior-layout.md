# Leopard 44 / Sunsail 444 — Interior Layout and Living Spaces

## Saloon

- Open-plan with 2.12m (~7ft) headroom
- Abundant natural light from vertical portlights and overhead hatches
- Large L-shaped dinette (forward starboard) seating 5–6 people
- Full-sized watertight door to forward cockpit for cross-ventilation
- Convertible saloon table for additional double berth

## Galley

- L-shaped (or U-shaped) in aft port corner of saloon
- Chef remains engaged with guests in both saloon and aft cockpit (via opening window)
- **Equipment:** 3-burner Force 10 gas stove/oven, double stainless steel sinks, Corian countertops
- **Refrigeration:** Cherrywood module with Vitrifrigo drawer-style front-opening fridge/freezer (~212L)

## Cabin Configurations

### 3-Cabin Owner's Version
- **Starboard hull (entire):** Private master suite
  - Queen-size berth aft
  - Amidships lounge: sofa + desk
  - Large head with separate shower (forward)
  - Dedicated washer/dryer locker
- **Port hull:** Two double cabins, each with en-suite head/shower

### 4-Cabin Charter Version (Sunsail 444)
- Mirrors port hull layout on both sides
- Four double cabins, each with en-suite head
- Forward cabins include single v-berth (child or storage)
- Accommodates up to 10 people total

## Cockpit Areas

### Forward Cockpit (Signature Feature)
- Accessed directly from saloon via watertight door
- L-shaped settee with small table
- Fixed overhead hardtop protection
- Private getaway at anchor; breezy social spot underway
- Excellent visibility and ventilation

### Aft Cockpit
- Primary social area, level with saloon floor
- Seating aft and to port
- Large table for up to 8 people
- Direct galley access via window

## Helm Station

- Raised starboard position with 360-degree visibility
- All sail controls (halyards, sheets) led to 3x Lewmar winches
- Single-handed sailing capable
- Protected by hardtop bimini with fixed port for sail viewing

## Storage

- **Interior:** In-floor storage bins, dedicated trash bins, hanging lockers, cupboards, drawers
- **Exterior:** Large bow lockers (house water tanks + optional generator)
- **Davits:** Electric davit system at transom for tender

## Layout Pros and Cons

| Pros | Cons |
|---|---|
| Forward cockpit — extra social space, superior ventilation | No dedicated nav station (use dinette) |
| Level flooring saloon-to-cockpit — safer movement | Owner's hull sofa seen as wasted space |
| Single-point sail handling from protected helm | Helm seat tight for two adults |
| Quiet interior (sound-insulated engine rooms + A/C) | Raised boom (hardtop) makes sail access harder |
