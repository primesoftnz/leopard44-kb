# Leopard 44 / Sunsail 444 — Ex-Charter Buying Guide

## Charter Wear Patterns

- **Refrigerator drawers:** Rust extensively within 6–8 months of charter service; jam or break
- **Patio-style sliding doors:** Jump off tracks under heavy use
- **Hardtop bimini:** Bolts inside uprights loosen over time; difficult to access; threatens mainsheet tackle integrity
- **Trampolines:** Heavy abuse from multiple adults; OEM nets have low openness, prone to blowing out
- **Coaming cores:** Saturated balsa cores discovered in high-load areas (mainsheet attachment) due to flexing and water ingress

## Critical Survey Inspection Points

1. **Windows, forward cockpit door, hatches:** Check for leaks (common failure points)
2. **Forestay and gooseneck fixing points:** Check condition
3. **Steering cables:** Inspect for wear
4. **Hull core:** Verify no delamination or balsa saturation from past impacts
5. **Sail drive seals:** Check oil color — milky/"ranch dressing" = failed lower seals
6. **Sail drive rubber fairing:** Check for detachment
7. **Bimini bolt torque:** Access inside uprights and verify tight

## Hull Osmosis Risk

Low risk by design:
- **Isophthalic gelcoat** + **vinylester resin** specifically chosen as osmosis barrier
- Vacuum-bonded balsa core with end-grain prevents moisture wicking
- Still worth checking in survey for any impact damage points

## Engine Hours (2015 Model)

- **Expected range:** 2,500–4,000 hours
- **Example:** 2015 Sunsail "Kitten Caboodle" had ~2,941 hours
- **Buyer preference:** Under 4,000 hours

## Sail Drive Condition Indicators

| Indicator | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Milky/latte oil | Lower seals failed, water ingress | Replace seals |
| Hard/stiff diaphragm | Normal after ~10 years | Replace preventatively (insurance requirement) |
| Rubber fairing detached/flapping | Common wear item | Replace with fiberglass "clamshell" |
| SD50 clutch cone issues | Known problem on older units | Consider SD60 upgrade |

## Rigging Age Concerns

- Insurance may deny mast failure claims with rigging >15 years old
- Tropical use: many owners replace standing rigging at ~9 years
- 2015 boat = 11 years old in 2026 — **rigging replacement likely needed**

## Sunsail Fleet Maintenance

- Engine services every 3 months during charter
- Phase-out refit at no charge to buyer (linens, cookware, safety gear)
- Basic "charter ready" condition — not optimized for private cruising

## Refit: Charter to Private Cruising

### Power Systems
- Replace AGM with 600Ah lithium banks
- Add 400W+ solar arrays on hardtop
- Upgrade inverter/charger (3,000W Victron Multiplus)

### Water
- Install watermaker (30–40 gal/hour)

### Navigation and Safety
- Add AIS, radar, long-range antennas
- Upgrade to current Raymarine Axiom chartplotters

### Sail Handling
- Install bowsprit for Code 0 / asymmetrical spinnaker
- Upgrade trampolines to 3/8" Offshore netting

### Comfort
- Washing machine (use dedicated locker in owner's version)
- Isinglass enclosures for helm and rear cockpit
- Caframo fans throughout
