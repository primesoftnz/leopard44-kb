## Factors Affecting Value

### Charter vs Private History
- **Owner's (3-cabin) versions** are highly sought after — sell quickly at or near full asking price
- **Ex-charter (4-cabin)** models priced ~$350,000; higher engine hours, less specialized equipment
- Charter boats "phased out" and refitted by Sunsail at no charge to buyer

### Engine Hours
- Buyers typically seek <4,000 engine hours
- 2015 ex-charter example (Kitten Caboodle): ~2,941 hours

### Equipment and Upgrades
- Private boats commonly include: lithium batteries (600Ah), AIS, radar, internet boosters, custom enclosures
- These upgrades add significant value over stripped charter configurations

### Damage History
- Hurricane damage, dismasting, or major repairs can reduce price significantly
- Example: 2015 model with hurricane repair + replaced mast listed at $340,000 (dropped $29K over 3 months)

## Depreciation Trends

- Catamarans: higher price per pound than monohulls, but comparable price per cubic foot of living space
- Leopards regarded as "exceptional value" and "solid investment" in brokerage market
- Charter fleet collaboration means boats are designed to minimize downtime and maintain long-term durability
- Resale varies by age, condition, competition, and local market
