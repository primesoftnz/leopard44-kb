# Leopard 44 / Sunsail 444 — Known Issues and Common Problems

## Structural and Design Issues

### Poor Windward Performance
The boat struggles to point well to weather, particularly when the jib is furled or when reefed. Described as "not comfortable to windward in big seas."

### Mainsheet Configuration
Early models delivered without a mainsheet traveler, making leech tension difficult. Later boats use a twin-sheet (Delta) system that many owners find "bizarre" and "infinitely more complicated" for tacking and jibing than a traditional traveler.

### Winch Clearance
The starboard winch at the helm station is positioned extremely close to the hard bimini support stanchion. Using a winch handle risks smashing fingers against the stanchion.

### Sliding Saloon Door
The sliding door between saloon and rear cockpit lacks a mechanism to hold it open. In heavy motion, it can slide violently back and forth (safety hazard). Patio-style doors also reported to jump off tracks.

### Anchor Positioning
The anchor hoists under the bridgedeck rather than all the way forward — some owners find this less than ideal.

### Interior Design Tradeoffs
- No dedicated navigation station (dinette used instead)
- Owner's version sofa in starboard hull criticized as wasting space that could be additional storage

## Equipment Failures and Maintenance

### Refrigerator Drawers
On Sunsail 444 models, fridge drawers rust extensively within 6–8 months. Drawers frequently jam or break.

### Sail Drive Seals and Diaphragms
- Failed lower seals indicated by "latte" or "cappuccino" colored oil (water ingress)
- Diaphragms become hard and stiff after ~10 years — replace as preventive maintenance
- External rubber fairing (boot) at hull bottom frequently detaches or flaps; often requires replacement or fiberglass reinforcement

### Trampoline Blowouts
OEM nets have low openness, catching significant water during hard upwind passages. Results in nets blowing out or hardware being pulled from the hull.

### Leaking Hatches
Often attributed to improper factory installation (unleveled mounting flange) rather than hatch defect.

## General Maintenance Items

- **Rigging:** Check forestay and gooseneck fixing points, steering cables before long passages
- **Engine upgrade:** Many owners recommend 39hp over standard 29hp for distance cruising
- **Hardtop bimini bolts:** Located inside uprights, difficult to access. Loosening threatens bimini and mainsheet tackle integrity
